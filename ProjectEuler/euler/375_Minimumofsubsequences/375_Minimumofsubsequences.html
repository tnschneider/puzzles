Minimum of subsequences

Problem 375

Published on Saturday, 10th March 2012, 10:00 pm; Solved by 517; Difficulty rating: 40%

Let S n be an integer sequence produced with the following pseudo-random number generator:

S 0 = 290797
S n +1 = S n 2 mod 50515093

Let A( i , j ) be the minimum of the numbers S i , S i +1 , ... , S j for i ≤ j .
Let M( N ) = ΣA( i , j ) for 1 ≤ i ≤ j ≤ N .
We can verify that M(10) = 432256955 and M(10 000) = 3264567774119.

Find M(2 000 000 000).
