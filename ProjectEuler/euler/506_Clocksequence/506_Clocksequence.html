Clock sequence

Problem 506

Published on Sunday, 8th March 2015, 04:00 am; Solved by 398; Difficulty rating: 30%

Consider the infinite repeating sequence of digits:
1234321234321234321...

Amazingly, you can break this sequence of digits into a sequence of integers such that the sum of the digits in the n 'th value is n .

The sequence goes as follows:
1, 2, 3, 4, 32, 123, 43, 2123, 432, 1234, 32123, ...

Let v n be the n 'th value in this sequence. For example, v 2 = 2, v 5 = 32 and v 11 = 32123.

Let S ( n ) be v 1 + v 2 + ... + v n . For example, S (11) = 36120, and S (1000) mod 123454321 = 18232686.

Find S (10 14 ) mod 123454321.
