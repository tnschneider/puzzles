Remainder of polynomial division

Problem 498

Published on Sunday, 18th January 2015, 07:00 am; Solved by 245; Difficulty rating: 40%

For positive integers n and m , we define two polynomials F n ( x ) = x n and G m ( x ) = ( x -1) m .
We also define a polynomial R n , m ( x ) as the remainder of the division of F n ( x ) by G m ( x ).
For example, R 6,3 ( x ) = 15 x 2 - 24 x + 10.

Let C( n , m , d ) be the absolute value of the coefficient of the d -th degree term of R n , m ( x ).
We can verify that C(6, 3, 1) = 24 and C(100, 10, 4) = 227197811615775.

Find C(10 13 , 10 12 , 10 4 ) mod 999999937.
