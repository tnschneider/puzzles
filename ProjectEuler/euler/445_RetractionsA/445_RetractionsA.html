Problem 445

Published on Saturday, 16th November 2013, 10:00 pm; Solved by 189; Difficulty rating: 50%

For every integer n>1, the family of functions f n,a,b is defined by f n,a,b ( x )≡a x +b mod n for a,b, x integer and 0<a<n, 0≤b<n, 0≤ x <n.
We will call f n,a,b a retraction if f n,a,b (f n,a,b ( x ))≡f n,a,b ( x ) mod n for every 0≤ x <n.
Let R(n) be the number of retractions for n.

You are given that
∑ R(c) for c=C(100 000,k), and 1 ≤ k ≤99 999 ≡628701600 (mod 1 000 000 007).
(C(n,k) is the binomial coefficient).

Find ∑ R(c) for c=C(10 000 000,k), and 1 ≤k≤ 9 999 999.
Give your answer modulo 1 000 000 007.
