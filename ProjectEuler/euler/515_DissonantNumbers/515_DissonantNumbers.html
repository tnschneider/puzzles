Dissonant Numbers

Problem 515

Published on Sunday, 10th May 2015, 07:00 am; Solved by 239; Difficulty rating: 40%

Let d ( p , n ,0) be the multiplicative inverse of n modulo prime p , defined as n × d ( p , n ,0) = 1 mod p .
Let d ( p , n , k ) = $\sum_{i=1}^n$ d ( p , i , k −1) for k ≥ 1.
Let D ( a , b , k ) = $\sum$( d ( p , p -1, k ) mod p ) for all primes a ≤ p < a + b .

You are given:

  • D (101,1,10) = 45
  • D (10 3 ,10 2 ,10 2 ) = 8334
  • D (10 6 ,10 3 ,10 3 ) = 38162302

Find D (10 9 ,10 5 ,10 5 ).
