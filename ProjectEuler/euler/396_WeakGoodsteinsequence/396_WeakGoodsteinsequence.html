Weak Goodstein sequence

Problem 396

Published on Sunday, 30th September 2012, 02:00 am; Solved by 380; Difficulty rating: 40%

For any positive integer n, the nth weak Goodstein sequence {g 1 , g 2 , g 3 , ...} is defined as:

  • g 1 = n
  • for k > 1, g k is obtained by writing g k -1 in base k , interpreting it as a base k + 1 number, and subtracting 1.
The sequence terminates when g k becomes 0.

For example, the 6th weak Goodstein sequence is {6, 11, 17, 25, ...}:

  • g 1 = 6.
  • g 2 = 11 since 6 = 110 2 , 110 3 = 12, and 12 - 1 = 11.
  • g 3 = 17 since 11 = 102 3 , 102 4 = 18, and 18 - 1 = 17.
  • g 4 = 25 since 17 = 101 4 , 101 5 = 26, and 26 - 1 = 25.
and so on.

It can be shown that every weak Goodstein sequence terminates.

Let G( n ) be the number of nonzero elements in the n th weak Goodstein sequence.
It can be verified that G(2) = 3, G(4) = 21 and G(6) = 381.
It can also be verified that ΣG( n ) = 2517 for 1 ≤ n < 8.

Find the last 9 digits of ΣG( n ) for 1 ≤ n < 16.
